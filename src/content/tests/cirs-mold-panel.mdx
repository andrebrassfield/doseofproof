## What This Test Measures

The CIRS Mold Panel is a group of specialized blood markers that evaluate the innate immune system's response to biotoxins. In a healthy individual, biotoxins are tagged by antibodies and eliminated. In 25% of the population (genetically susceptible), the biotoxins remain invisible to the adaptive immune system, causing the innate immune system to spin out of control, releasing highly inflammatory cytokines.

This panel measures:
- **TGF-Beta 1:** An immune signaling cytokine.
- **C4a (Complement Split Product):** Reflects rapid activation of the complement pathway.
- **MSH (Melanocyte-Stimulating Hormone):** A regulator of hormones, sleep, and gut integrity.
- **MMP-9:** Indicates deep tissue inflammation and barrier permeability.
- **VIP (Vasoactive Intestinal Peptide):** Regulates pulmonary artery pressure and brain volume.

## Why Doctors Skip It

Most conventional doctors are unfamiliar with the Shoemaker Protocol for CIRS. They will run basic inflammatory markers like **hs-CRP** or **ESR**, which will come back normal because biotoxins do not activate the adaptive immune pathways measured by CRP.

- **High TGF-Beta 1:** Avoid strenuous exercise as it drives further elevation. Use natural immune modulators like Boswellia and molecular hydrogen.
- **High MMP-9:** Avoid high-amylose diets (wheat, potatoes, corn) to limit carbohydrate-driven inflammation.
- **Low MSH:** Prioritize strict circadian sleep hygiene and support mucosal barriers in the gut.

## v1.0 — The first real protocol (2025-03-22)

v1.0 is the baseline. Everything in v1.1, v2.0, and v2.1 iterates on what v1.0 got right and what it got wrong.

### What it was

- **Itraconazole pulse therapy.** Two weeks on, two weeks off, rotating with another antifungal. Prescription-only.
- **Cholestyramine.** Bile acid binder, prescription. Twice daily, away from food.
- **OTC binders.** Activated charcoal and chlorella, no sequencing — taken when remembered.
- **Standard blood work every 90 days.** CBC, CMP, thyroid, lipids. The "normal" panels.
- **Functional medicine intake.** Two-hour initial consult, supplement protocol, no imaging.

### What it got right

- It was a real protocol. Until v1.0, the work was a list of supplements taken inconsistently with no measurement. v1.0 was the first version that someone other than me could look at and understand the sequence.

### What it got wrong

- **No sequencing.** The binders were taken randomly. Antifungals were pulsed but drainage was not supported. Herxheimer reactions were common and unmanaged.
- **No imaging.** The structural root cause (cervical curve, C1-C2) was not investigated for 18 months.
- **No mast cell stabilization.** Histamine reactivity was treated as a downstream symptom rather than an amplifier of every other protocol.

### Why it's still here

Even a flawed v1.0 has value. It defines the baseline. Every change in v1.1, v2.0, and v2.1 can be measured against what v1.0 was doing, and the diff tells the story.

### The line that matters

> "Not selling. Just proving." — Dose of Proof

v1.0 is the proof that documenting a protocol (even an imperfect one) beats keeping it in your head.

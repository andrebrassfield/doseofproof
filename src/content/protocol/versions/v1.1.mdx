## v1.1 — Binder sequencing + mast cell stabilizers (2025-08-10)

The v1.0 antifungal rotation was producing more side effects than recoveries. This version dropped the prescription antifungals entirely in favor of an over-the-counter binder sequence + a daily mast cell stabilizer stack.

### What changed

- **Removed antifungal rotation.** Pulse-dose prescription antifungals were producing Herxheimer reactions severe enough to wipe out a week of recovery each cycle.
- **OTC binder sequencing.** Activated charcoal (morning, away from food), chlorella (midday), bentonite clay (evening). Three binders, three timing windows, no overlap.
- **Mast cell stabilizer stack.** Quercetin (500 mg) + vitamin C (1000 mg) twice daily. Stabilizes mast cell degranulation so the binder die-off doesn't get amplified by histamine release.

### What I tracked

Ochratoxin dropped from 28.4 ppb to 11.2 ppb over 4 months. Symptom flare events (days where the overall symptom load spiked) went from 8-12 per month to 3-4 per month. Both numbers were published in [the mycotoxin test guide](/tests/mycotoxin-urine-test).

### What's still broken

- Sleep onset latency (45+ min, down from 90 but still bad)
- Resting heart rate (76 bpm average, target 65)
- Sinus pressure on waking

These carry into v2.0.

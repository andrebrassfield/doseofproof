## v2.0 — Structural imaging becomes primary (2026-02-01)

Until v2.0, the protocol treated cervical imaging as a downstream check — something you did after environmental, autonomic, and inflammatory work had failed to produce an answer. v2.0 flipped the order: structural imaging goes first whenever the symptom cluster includes neck-position-dependent changes.

### What changed

- **Upright dynamic X-ray (flexion + extension).** Standard supine MRI misses C1-C2 instability. Upright + dynamic catches the alar ligament compromise that supine imaging hides.
- **TyTron C-3000 self-screen.** Paraspinal thermography is cheap, radiation-free, and surfaces thermal asymmetries that correlate with autonomic guarding. It's a pre-screen, not a diagnosis.
- **Sequencing rule.** If the patient has any structural symptom (positional brain fog, posture-dependent heart rate spikes, occipital pressure), structural imaging goes first. Environmental testing waits.

### Why it matters

The CIRS markers I had previously been chasing kept coming back "elevated" without any clear environmental trigger. Once the cervical imaging confirmed C1-C2 translation, the whole symptom stack resolved into a cleaner picture: mechanical irritation of the vagus nerve → autonomic dysregulation → mast cell instability → inflammatory cascade. Treating that cascade downstream without addressing the mechanical driver was a multi-year detour.

### Lesson

Diagnostic sequencing is a leverage point. Imaging the right thing first can collapse a 14-month protocol into a 4-month one.
